CFPB Data Shows Nearly 35,000 Compaints Against Navient Over 9-Year Period

By Daniel Stein

The number of complaints against Navient are heavily correlated with the status of the various lawsuits against Navient. The Consumer Financial Protections Bureau filed a lawsuit against Navient in January 2017, a year that saw over 12,000 complaints, the highest of any year observed in the graph. The number of complaints, which decreaesed from its peak in 2017 until spiking again in 2024, stayed over 3,000 for the next two years as attorneys general from a host of states filed their own lawsuits against Navient. When the news broke that 39 attorneys general settled their suit against Navient in 2022, the next year saw the lowest total number of complaints (1376) in the range.



As observed in the graph above, the most drastic shift in the number of complaints took place between 2016 and 2017. 2017 saw a 261% increase in complaints, by far the largest change observed between years. After 2017, the number of complaints continued to decrease between 2018 and 2021, as indicated by the negative percent change values. It was also between 2018 and 2021 that the number of complaints did not change drastically, as indicated by the plateauing line in the graph above. Also worth noting is the 124% increase in complaints between 2023 and 2024, only the 3rd year that saw a positive percent change across the 8 year span, and the biggest change, positive or negative, since 2017.
